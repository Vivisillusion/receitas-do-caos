4. Brazilian pancake

This recipe was passed down to me by my best friend, who learned it from her mother, it was a Sunday tradition in their home. The scent of it alone feels like childhood in a frying pan.

Ingredients

  • 1 mug (or about 240ml) of milk
  • 1 mug of all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon of oil (plus more for the pan)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Your choice of filling: shredded meat, cheese and ham, cream cheese, butter, or whatever your wicked heart desires

Instructions

  1. In a blender, combine the milk, flour, egg, oil, and salt. Blend until the batter is smooth.
  2. Heat a non-stick frying pan until it’s very hot — this is crucial. A properly heated pan is the secret to pancakes that don’t stick.
  3. Lightly grease the pan with oil. Pour a ladleful of batter and tilt the pan to spread it thinly and evenly.
  4. Cook until the edges lift and the bottom is golden. Flip and cook the other side.
  5. If the pancake begins to stick, add more oil. This recipe needs a generous amount of oil throughout — embrace it.
  6. Serve hot, stuffed with anything that feels like comfort. You deserve it.
