7. Tasteless Bread

This bread is all about form over flavor. It’s soft, golden, and absolutely stunning in a cake or bread pan! But... it’s intentionally bland. That’s not a flaw, that’s a feature. It becomes the perfect canvas for fillings like raisins, cheese, or pepperoni. Or just bake it plain, slice it warm, and admire its quiet beauty.

Ingredients

  • 360 ml of milk
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 3 mugs of all-purpose flour (no baking powder)
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 10g of active dry yeast

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, heat the milk with the butter until the butter fully melts, use it hot!
  2. In a large bowl, mix the dry ingredients, the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast.
  3. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients, like digging a little grave, and pour in the eggs and warm milk-butter mixture.
  4. Stir everything together. The batter should be liquid, but thick enough that when you stir it, you see the bottom of the bowl for a second.
  5. Cover the bowl with a clean towel and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) for at least 10 minutes.
  7. Grease a loaf pan or cake tin with butter and lightly dust it with flour.
  8. Pour in the batter and bake for about 45 minutes, or until it’s puffed and golden.
